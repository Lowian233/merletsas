Who are we?

Merlet is the result of the union of three family companies. Driven by passion and commitment, we bring the best of Colombian citrus to the world.

Our experience and values allow us to ensure the highest quality in every fruit, from cultivation to commercialization.

With a sustainable and responsible approach, we work closely with our producers to ensure a harvest that not only meets the highest standards but also respects the environment and the well-being of local communities.

At Merlet, we are proud to be a benchmark in the industry, offering fresh, natural, and flavorful products that represent the essence of our land.

Our History

The citrus journey began in 1980 under the name "Cítricos Balmoral", with 40 hectares planted with Valencia Orange, Native Orange, Tangelo, Tahiti Lime, and Common Lime. It was our father, Gustavo Eastman Vélez, who took the first step on this path, laying the foundation for what is today a consolidated company in the agricultural sector.

In 2009, it transformed into a partnership, adopting the name INVERSIONES EL REFUGIO GEV S.C.A. Currently, we have more than 220 hectares of Tahiti Lime, about 120 hectares of Orange, and a total of 145 hectares planted, of which 60% are productive trees and the remaining 40% are of varying ages with annual plantings. Orange and tangelo trees represent 45% of the total, while lime trees make up 55%. The annual production is 3,500 tons, with an average of 50 tons per hectare of oranges and 35 tons per hectare of limes. In total, 29,000 trees are planted on completely flat land.

The Hacienda El Refugio estate, located 49 km from Villavicencio via Puerto López (44 km) and turning right (Estadero La María) 5 km towards the community of Puerto Colombia, covers 360 hectares. The annual planting plan is 20 hectares, and 35 hectares are dedicated to natural forest, which serves as the environmental lung of the area.

In 2019, the second generation of our family joined forces with the aim of taking the next step in the evolution of the estate. We created the company Merlet, which is now responsible for packaging and exporting our fruit, bringing the quality of our citrus to international markets.

By the year 2025, we had more than 200 hectares planted with Tahiti Lime and over 100 hectares of Orange. To date, we have exported more than 200 containers to markets in the United States and Europe, consolidating our international presence.

Citrus, through our dedication, innovation, and commitment, remain a part of Colombia's agricultural future.
